About Arbstack

Arbstack is a market-intelligence layer that helps users understand where markets disagree — before they bet, trade, or predict.

The Problem

Sports betting and prediction markets all price the same events, but they speak different languages.

  • Prediction markets quote probabilities in cents or percentages
  • Sportsbooks quote American odds with built-in margin (vig)
  • Fans are forced to compare numbers that were never designed to align

As a result, users see odds — but they do not see confidence gaps, bias, or mispricing. The most valuable information lives in the disagreement between markets, and it is usually invisible.

Arbstack’s Insight

Disagreement itself is information.

When independent markets price the same outcome differently, at least one of them is wrong — or early. Arbstack does not predict outcomes. It surfaces where confidence diverges so users can ask better questions.

The EPL Board

The English Premier League page is the first live demonstration of Arbstack. For every match, it shows three independent market views side-by-side:

  • Kalshi — prediction market pricing shown as team-vs-team probability (cents)
  • Polymarket — crowd-driven three-way probabilities (win / draw / loss)
  • SportsLine — bookmaker odds converted into clean split probabilities

All formats are normalized so they can be compared directly. No odds are hidden. No numbers are averaged away.

What Users Learn

Before placing a bet or making a prediction, a user can immediately see:

  • Which matches markets broadly agree on
  • Which matches show strong disagreement
  • Whether bookmakers or crowds are more confident
  • Where bias, overreaction, or delayed information may exist

Arbstack shifts the question from:

“Who should I bet on?”

to:

“Why do these markets disagree — and who is most likely wrong?”

What Arbstack Is — and Is Not

  • Not a betting platform
  • Not a tipster or prediction engine
  • Not an odds aggregator

Arbstack is a decision-quality tool. It makes uncertainty explicit and lets users decide how to act.

Vision

Arbstack’s long-term vision is to become the place users visit each day to ask:

“Where does the market disagree today — and why?”

Sports are the starting point. The same framework applies to politics, economics, and any domain where probabilities are traded and belief diverges from reality.
